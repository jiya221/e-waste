Environmental Damage

Many electronic products contain toxic materials such as lead, mercury, and cadmium. When dumped in landfills, these chemicals can leak into soil and water supplies.

Air Pollution

In some countries, electronic waste is burned to recover valuable metals. Burning plastics and electronic components releases toxic chemicals into the air, contributing to air pollution and climate change.

Wildlife Impact

Toxic chemicals from e-waste can enter rivers and oceans. Fish, birds, and other wildlife may consume these pollutants, affecting their health and the ecosystems they live in.

Loss of Valuable Resources

Throwing electronic devices into landfill wastes valuable materials including gold, silver, copper, aluminium, and rare earth metals. Recycling allows these materials to be recovered and reused in new products, reducing the need for mining.

Health Risks

People who handle e-waste without proper protection may be exposed to dangerous chemicals. Burning electronic waste can also release harmful fumes into the air.

Data Security Issues

Old phones and computers often contain personal information. Devices should be wiped properly before disposal to prevent identity theft and data breaches.
